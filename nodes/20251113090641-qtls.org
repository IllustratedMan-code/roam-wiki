#+title: Quantitative Trait Loci

A region in the genome where variation of said region effects some
trait that can be quantified.

For example, a "binding" QTL might be a
region where binding of a protein (measured with ChIP-seq) is altered
when variation in that region occurs.

Identifying QTLs requires multiple samples with the same quantitative measurements
(i.e. ChIP-seq) that are genetically similar, but not identical. 


QTLs are a standard in [[id:d1d9fda3-fb43-4888-a179-7f24ad0554eb][bioinformatics]] analysis.
